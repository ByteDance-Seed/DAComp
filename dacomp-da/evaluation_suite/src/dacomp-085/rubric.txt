  ## Requirement 3: Diagnosis of Work Characteristic Differences and Input-Output Imbalance (Up to 12 points)

  ### Standard 3.1: Input vs. Output Measurement and Imbalance Identification (Up to 6 points)

  #### Path 3.1.A | Output/Input Ratio (ratio) Method
  - Sub-standard 3.1.A.1 (Completeness | 1 pt): Define output_input_ratio = combined_output / input_score, where input_score = avg_weekly_hours/40 + 0.5×shift_flag + retention_score (Low=0.25, Medium=0.5, High=0.75, Very High=1.0).
  - Sub-standard 3.1.A.2 (Accuracy | 4 pts): For the High/Very High subset, provide overall Q25 ≈ 16.817 and Q75 ≈ 23.070 (tolerance ≤1%). There are 165 low-ratio job profiles (ratio < Q25).   - Sub-standard 3.1.A.3 (Conclusion | 1 pt): Explain the structural contradiction of "heavy input, light output" for low-ratio positions and the necessity for governance.

  #### Path 3.1.B | Input Burden - Output Score (imbalance) Method
  - Sub-standard 3.1.B.1 (Completeness | 1 pt): Construct output_norm = 0.6×(job_health/100) + 0.4×((avg_career_development−1)/4), input_burden = 0.4×saturation + 0.3×(avg_weekly_hours/40) + 0.1×shift_flag + 0.2×retention_score, and imbalance_score = input_burden − output_norm.
  - Sub-standard 3.1.B.2 (Accuracy | 4 pts): Reproduce overall avg_imbalance ≈ 0.1354, median ≈ 0.1299. For the Top 25 imbalanced job profiles: avg_imbalance ≈ 0.499, avg_weekly_hours ≈ 47.09, shift rate ≈ 84%, Very High retention=100% (tolerance ≤0.01/1pp).
  - Sub-standard 3.1.B.3 (Conclusion | 1 pt): Explain that the Top 25 are driven by a combination of shift work and 'Very High' retention difficulty, where the input-side burden far exceeds the output.

  #### Path 3.1.C | Saturation × Output Quadrant (Reduce vs Invest)
  - Sub-standard 3.1.C.1 (Completeness | 1 pt): Using the median of output_norm ≈0.656 as a boundary, define reduce = saturation>1.0 & output_norm<median, and invest = saturation<0.6 & output_norm>median.
  - Sub-standard 3.1.C.2 (Accuracy | 4 pts): Report 53 'reduce' jobs (avg_sat ≈ 1.117, avg_out ≈ 0.571, avg_tqi ≈ 0.0545), 115 'invest' jobs (avg_sat ≈ 0.406, avg_out ≈ 0.725, avg_tqi ≈ 0.0590), and 492 'other' jobs (tolerance ≤1%).
